## Session 1: Problem Interview

This session is a Problem Interview.
Your only goal is to understand the respondent's
real-world problems, behaviors, constraints,
and existing workarounds.
Do NOT validate solutions, features, product ideas,
pricing, or willingness to pay.
Stay 100% focused on specific past events,
not opinions or ideas about the future.

---

## Session 1 Count Check

Before returning the final JSON, verify:
- icebreakers: exactly 3
- context questions: 1~2
- problems questions: 2~3
- alternatives questions: 1~2
- wtp questions: 0
- total core questions: 6~7

Session 1 must NOT include any question
with section = "wtp".

If any count is wrong, adjust the question list
(by merging or removing lower-priority ones)
BEFORE returning the final JSON.
Never exceed the total core question limit.

---

## Session 1 Section + Intent Mapping

Use these intent patterns by section:

- context:
  - "recent_workflow"

- problems:
  - "recent_pain_event"
  - "pain_severity"
  - "pain_frequency"

- alternatives:
  - "current_workaround"
  - "alternative_usage"
  - "switch_trigger"

Use "objection_probe" only when exploring
a past hesitation, delay, or avoidance related
to a real-world action the respondent actually faced.

Do NOT use Session 2+ intents in Session 1:
"decision_process", "value_perception",
"budget_signal", "wtp_probe"

---

## Session 1 Priority Rules

When in doubt about question focus, always ask:
"What did this person actually do the last time
 they faced this situation?"

Never ask:
- what they plan to do
- what they wish existed
- what they would pay for
- whether they like the product idea
- whether they would use the founder's product

Every question should help uncover one of these:
- recent real context
- actual workflow
- painful moment
- consequence
- workaround
- alternative behavior
- unresolved gap

---

## Session 1 Report Bucket Defaults

Assign report_bucket primarily by section:
- icebreaker → "context"
- context → "context"
- problems → "top_pain"
- alternatives → "alternatives"

Use "next_action_signal" when the question
is explicitly designed to uncover what the founder
should try next — a segment change, new experiment,
or stopping a direction.

Use "hypothesis_signal" only when the question
directly tests the founder's riskiest assumption
and does not fit cleanly into context,
top_pain, or alternatives.

Never use "wtp_signal" in Session 1.
