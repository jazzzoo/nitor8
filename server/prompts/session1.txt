Session: 1 - Problem Interview

Rules you MUST follow without exception:
- NEVER mention any solution, product, feature, idea, or future possibility.
- ONLY talk about current problems, past experiences, frustrations, and workarounds.
- All questions MUST be open-ended, non-leading, focused on past behavior or current reality.
- No yes/no questions. No hypothetical "would you" questions.
- Prioritize revealing: emotional impact, time/money lost, frequency, current coping methods.

---

## Session 1 Purpose
Validate whether the customer actually experiences this problem and how severe it is.
The goal is to confirm or disprove the founder's hypothesis.

## Strictly Prohibited
- Never mention solutions, products, features, or ideas
- No hypothetical "what if this existed?" scenarios
- No payment intent or pricing questions (covered in Session 4)
- No MVP or prototype questions (covered in Session 3)
- No leading questions like "Wasn't that inconvenient?"
- No closed yes/no questions

## Required Exploration Areas
1. Current behavior patterns: How does the customer handle this problem now?
2. Frequency and intensity: How often and how severely?
3. Current alternatives: What methods/tools/services are they using?
4. Limitations of alternatives: What's unsatisfying about current solutions?
5. Consequences: What actual losses (time/money/emotion) does this problem cause?

## Section-Intent Assignment (REQUIRED — assign section + intent to every question)

context section (2~3 questions):
  - intent options: "recent_workflow", "role_context"
  - Goal: understand current situation, role, and daily workflow before exploring problems

problems section (3~4 questions):
  - intent options: "recent_pain_event", "pain_frequency", "pain_severity"
  - Goal: identify specific pain points, how often they happen, and their emotional/financial impact

alternatives section (2~3 questions):
  - intent options: "current_workaround", "alternative_usage", "switch_trigger"
  - Goal: understand what solutions they currently use and why those fall short

wtp section (2~3 questions):
  - intent options: "wtp_probe", "budget_signal", "value_perception"
  - Goal: explore implicit willingness to invest in a better solution (no pricing anchors)

## Required Comment Elements
Each question's "why" field must include:
- Connection to lean customer development principles
- Expected pain intensity criteria
- Empathy reaction example (e.g. "That must have been really hard, could you tell me more?")

## Quality Validation
After generating, self-verify:
- Zero solution mentions (fix immediately if violated)
- At least 3 "why" questions in follow-ups
- 70%+ of questions request specific examples/timeframes
- context: ≥2 questions with section="context"
- problems: ≥3 questions with section="problems"
- alternatives: ≥2 questions with section="alternatives"
- wtp: ≥2 questions with section="wtp"
- Every question must have valid section, intent, report_bucket, id fields
- No duplicate themes (failure/emotion)
